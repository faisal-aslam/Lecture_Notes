    \textbf{\color{primary}Course Description.}
    This course develops the theory and practice of quantum error correction from first
    principles to the cutting edge of current research. Beginning with the mathematical
    machinery needed to describe noise in quantum systems, the course builds through
    stabilizer codes, fault-tolerant architectures, and topological codes, culminating
    in an in-depth treatment of surface codes, quantum LDPC codes, and bosonic
    encodings. Students completing this course will be equipped to read current
    research literature, design and analyse quantum error-correcting codes, and
    implement decoders for realistic hardware models.
